🧂 Electrolytes ("snake juice")

Daily mix for fasting days — stir into 2 L water and sip across the whole day, never chug:
· ½ tsp table salt (sodium)
· 1 tsp potassium chloride ("No-Salt" / "Lite Salt")
· ½ tsp baking soda
· ¼–½ tsp food-grade Epsom salt (magnesium) — optional, start low
Today's electrolyte check
This is the popular fasting-community recipe, not a prescription. Do not exceed the potassium amount — too much is genuinely dangerous, especially with kidney issues or potassium-sparing meds (including spironolactone, common for PCOS). Unsure? Plain salted water + a doctor's opinion beats internet recipes.

📏 Measurements

🌊 Why the scale jumps (and why it isn't fat)
The scale can swing ±2 kg overnight, and almost none of it is fat. The usual suspects: a salty meal (the body holds water for a day or two) · carbs after a low-carb stretch (every gram of stored glycogen carries 3–4 g of water — this is fuel, not fat) · a late or large meal still in transit · the week before a period (hormonal water, often 1–2 kg) · new or hard exercise (muscles hold water while they repair) · travel, poor sleep, or stress. Actual fat change needs roughly 7,700 kcal per kg — a single day simply can't do that in either direction. The gold line on the chart is the truth; single days are weather. 💙
The tape often moves before the scale does — that's why this tab exists. 💙

What is Lífsteinn?

A private companion for witnessing your days — named for the life stone every Hverfolk carries, from first breath to last.

It weaves together: food, recipes & fasting · movement, habits & goals · cycle & body · sleep & dreams · a journal kept on a calendar of Moons. Every thread is optional — any of them can be tucked away on the Loom tab.

What it does: lives entirely on this device · works offline · exports backups & spreadsheets that are yours to keep · never punishes a missed day. There is always room at the loom.

What it doesn't do: no accounts, no cloud, no ads, no feeds — and no medical advice. Lífsteinn witnesses; your doctor advises.
